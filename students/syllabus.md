### Approach to class and expectations

The course will be organized in a modular fashion, with some guest lectures. Each machine learning method will be studied as it is applied to a physical problem, based on open data and literature examples. Each session may include lecture on AI and physics topics, or labs (typically coding based), or, most commonly, a mixture of the two,

Don't worry about how much you already know, especially do not compare it to what other students know. You may have the wrong perception of your skills, and of the skills of your classmates, and your strengths and the strengths of your background may be less obvious than, say coding or math, but just as important for a Natural Scientist. Some of you may have a good handle and understanding of some or all of the physics problems we will study, others a good handle on coding, others yet an easy time understanding the details of the analysis. All these components make proficiency in this class, and all these components make a good scientist. We, the class assistants and I, are here to help you develop the skills you do not yet have and strengthen the skills you already have.

This class is in person. Some lectures will be online to accommodate instructor’s travel.

## 2. Course Description

This course teaches the basics of data-driven inference in the natural sciences. Students will learn examples of machine learning methods applied to current problems in Physics and the Natural Sciences, acquire basic computational skills, knowledge of statistical analysis, error analysis, good and ethical practices for handling, processing, and analyzing data programmatically, and communication and visualization skills. Some of the simpler algorithms are explored in detail and implemented from scratch, others are implemented through the use of dedicated python libraries. The course also uses industry-ready tools (e.g. Python libraries, Git).

### Prerequisites

Some coding experience, preferably in python, is expected.

## 3. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this class, you should be able to formulate an appropriate analysis plan for a research question, select, gather, and prepare data for analysis, and choose and apply machine learning methods to the data.

Students will have a strong grasp of statistical analysis including an understanding of frequentist and Bayesian frameworks

Students will have an understanding of AI methodology and modern frameworks for the extraction of information from data pertaining physical and natural systems.

Students will have a deep understanding of the scientific method and best practices for formulating hypothesis, treating data, interpreting and communicating analysis results.

- Use student engagement metrics and qualitative feedback to capture insights beyond test scores. (participation, continuous feedback on quizzes that assess conceptual understanding and ensure students keep up with the material throughout the semester)
